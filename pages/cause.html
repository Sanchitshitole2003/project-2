CAUSE OF POLLUTION

Air-Pollution

• Emission from Vehicles: Exhaust gases from cars, trucks, and other vehicles release pollutants into the air.

• Industrial Activities: Factories and power plants emit pollutants like sulfur dioxide and particulate matter.

• Agricultural Practices: Pesticides and fertilizers release harmful chemicals into the atmosphere.

• Deforestation: Removal of trees reduces the earth's capacity to filter pollutants.

Water-Pollution

• Industrial Discharge: Chemicals and waste products from industries can contaminate water bodies.

• Agricultural Runoff: Excess fertilizers and pesticides can seep into rivers and lakes.

• Improper Waste Disposal: Trash and sewage dumped into water bodies can lead to contamination.

• Oil Spills: Accidental release of oil into oceans or rivers harms aquatic life.

Soil-Pollution

• Improper Waste Disposal: Dumping of hazardous waste in soil can lead to contamination.

• Industrial Activities: Chemical leaks and improper disposal of waste can pollute soil.

• Mining Activities: Mining releases heavy metals and chemicals into the soil.

• Overuse of Pesticides and Fertilizers: Excessive use of agricultural chemicals can harm soil quality.

Noise-Pollution

• Traffic Noise: Vehicles, airplanes, and construction activities generate excessive noise.

• Industrial Noise: Factories and machinery produce high levels of noise pollution.

• Urbanization: Growing cities can lead to increased noise pollution from various sources.

• Recreational Activities: Loud music, events, and fireworks contribute to noise pollution

Light-Pollution

• Over-Illumination: Excessive outdoor lighting interferes with natural nightscapes.

• Glare: Excessive brightness causes discomfort and reduces visibility.

• Skyglow: Light scattered in the atmosphere creates a brightening of the night sky.

• Light Trespass: Light spilling over into areas where it's not needed or wanted.

Plastic-Pollution

• Single-Use Plastics: Widespread use of single-use plastics leads to accumulation in the environment.

• Improper Disposal: Improperly discarded plastics end up in oceans, harming marine life.

• Microplastics: Tiny plastic particles, often from degraded larger plastics, contaminate ecosystems.

• Plastic Production: The production process of plastic materials generates pollution.

Climate Change

• Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Burning fossil fuels releases CO2 and other greenhouse gases.

• Deforestation: Clearing forests reduces their carbon-absorbing capacity.

• Methane Emissions: Agriculture, landfills, and livestock produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

• Industrial Processes: Some industrial activities release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.
